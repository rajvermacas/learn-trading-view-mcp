Task: Create a complete, fully functional TradingView strategy script (strategy()) based on a specific "Double Bollinger Band" system. The script must include visual plots, clear entry/exit logic, risk management, and dynamic support/resistance (supply/demand) calculations.

1. Indicator Setup (The "Double B" Framework)
The strategy relies exclusively on two specific Bollinger Bands:

Bollinger Band 1 (The "1B" or Fast Band):

Period (Length): 4

Standard Deviation (Multiplier): 4

Source: open

Visuals: Plot the upper and lower bands in Red.

Bollinger Band 2 (The Standard Band):

Period (Length): 20

Standard Deviation (Multiplier): 2

Source: close

Visuals: Plot the upper, lower, and basis (middle) bands in White.

2. Dynamic Support/Resistance (Supply & Demand Zones)
The strategy requires knowing if price is interacting with a previous high or low to validate breakouts and reversals.

Implement a Pivot High and Pivot Low lookback system (e.g., ta.pivothigh and ta.pivotlow with a configurable left/right bars length, default to 10).

Store the most recent Pivot High as the current "Resistance / Supply Zone".

Store the most recent Pivot Low as the current "Support / Demand Zone".

3. State Definitions & Triggers
To execute a trade, price must interact with both Bollinger Bands simultaneously.

Upper Touch: The current candle's high is >= the Upper 1B AND >= the Upper Standard BB.

Lower Touch: The current candle's low is <= the Lower 1B AND <= the Lower Standard BB.

4. Entry Logic & Algorithms
Implement the following three entry conditions:

Condition A: Reversal Type 1 (The Wick Rejection)

Short Entry: An Upper Touch occurs. The candle closes below the Upper Standard BB, leaving a clear upper wick (indicating failure to make a new high).

Long Entry: A Lower Touch occurs. The candle closes above the Lower Standard BB, leaving a clear lower wick (indicating failure to make a new low).

Condition B: Reversal Type 2 (Failed Breakout at Resistance/Support)

Short Entry: An Upper Touch occurs, and the candle closes above both upper bands. HOWEVER, the high of this candle is <= the current Pivot High (Resistance). The very next candle closes bearish (close < open). Enter Short on the close of the bearish candle.

Long Entry: A Lower Touch occurs, and the candle closes below both lower bands. HOWEVER, the low of this candle is >= the current Pivot Low (Support). The very next candle closes bullish (close > open). Enter Long on the close of the bullish candle.

Condition C: The Real Breakout (Trend Continuation)

Long Entry: An Upper Touch occurs. The candle cleanly breaks and closes above both upper bands AND it closes above the current Pivot High (Resistance zone cleared). This is a confirmed breakout with acceptance.

Short Entry: A Lower Touch occurs. The candle cleanly breaks and closes below both lower bands AND it closes below the current Pivot Low (Support zone cleared).

5. Exit & Risk Management Logic
Traders need a defined risk-to-reward ratio (aiming for roughly 1:3).

Take Profit (Target):

For Longs: Target the Upper Standard BB (20-period).

For Shorts: Target the Lower Standard BB (20-period).

(Note: Because the bands are dynamic, the TP should be evaluated bar-by-bar, or you can code a fixed TP based on the band's value at entry).

Stop Loss:

For Reversals (Type 1 & 2): Place the stop loss just beyond the extreme wick of the entry setup (e.g., slightly above the high for shorts, slightly below the low for longs).

For Breakouts: Place the stop loss back inside the band (e.g., if long, stop loss triggers if price closes back below the Upper Standard BB).
