## Sponsorship Reasoning

**Why Sponsorship Is Present Or Not:** Present but moderate. Arvind benefits from two simultaneous real-world policy catalysts — India-US bilateral tariff at 18% (effective Feb 2026, below Bangladesh at 20%) and India-EU FTA (signed Jan 27, 2026, zero duty on textiles into EU's Rs 22.9 lakh crore import market). These are not speculative narratives; they are signed agreements with effective dates directly improving Arvind's export competitiveness. Business is operationally executing with revenue +13-14% YoY and denim/garment volumes at multi-year highs. However, "Moderate" rather than "Strong" because: (a) cotton input costs have reversed from below-MSP tailwind to Rs 60,500/candy headwind (+8.5% MoM), creating margin compression risk; (b) earnings quality impure — consolidated Q3 FY26 net profit fell 5% despite 14% revenue growth; (c) ROCE 13%/ROE 10.1% reflect capital-intensive business that converts growth to returns inefficiently; (d) price has consolidated flat near 52W highs since prior review, suggesting catalysts are largely priced in.

**Recent Trigger And What Likely Drove The Move:** The original 3-month move (Jan-Apr 2026) was driven precisely by the India-EU FTA (signed Jan 27, 2026) and India-US tariff reduction (50% to 18%, effective Feb 2026). Stock rallied from ~Rs 275 base to ~Rs 387-392. Since the Apr 19 prior review, price has moved sideways (Rs 386-392 range). New corporate action: UAE subsidiary Arvind Atelier (FZC) incorporated Apr 20 for textile trading (80% stake, AED 120K — immaterial). Leadership appointments Apr 3 (Nigam Shah ED, Pritesh Shah CS). No new order wins or guidance changes announced. The original catalysts are aging without fresh reinforcement.

**Business And Financial Evidence:** Revenue trajectory strong: FY25 Rs 8,390 Cr, Q3 FY26 Rs 2,373 Cr (+13.6% YoY, all-time high quarterly revenue). Denim volumes 51.6M meters (+8% YoY), garments 37.2M pieces (+16% YoY, 12-quarter high). FY25 EBITDA Rs 919 Cr at 11% margin, Q4 FY25 EBITDA 12.4% (16-quarter best). Profit trajectory uneven: Q1 FY26 Rs 54.7 Cr (+25% YoY), Q2 FY26 Rs 103.4 Cr (+73% YoY), Q3 FY26 Rs 101 Cr (-5% YoY, exceptional Rs 23.56 Cr labour code). FCF Rs 696 Cr FY25 with capex Rs 262 Cr. D/E 0.42x, Net Debt/EBITDA 1.4x. Market cap Rs 10,160 Cr. India's overall textile export growth FY 2025-26 was only 2.1%, meaning Arvind's 14% revenue growth significantly outperforms the sector.

**Catalyst Context:** Three structural tailwinds remain operative: (1) India-US 18% tariff positions India below Bangladesh (20%) and well below China (30%) — directly material given Arvind's 38% US revenue exposure; (2) India-EU FTA zero duty opens EU's Rs 22.9 lakh crore textile import market, with ICRA projecting 20-25% YoY export growth once operationalized; (3) China+1 and Bangladesh+1 sourcing diversification by global brands favours vertically integrated Indian producers. One prior tailwind has flipped: cotton prices have risen sharply to Rs 60,500/candy (+8.5% MoM), driven by structural deficit (output ~291 lakh bales vs mill consumption ~312 lakh bales). USDA projects 7% cotton production increase for MY 2026/27, but near-term supply remains tight. This is a meaningful deterioration from the prior dossier's "cotton below MSP" framing.

**Why The Rally Looks Legit Or Fragile For 1-8 Weeks:** The original rally (Jan-Apr 2026) was legitimate — anchored in signed trade agreements with verifiable dates. For the next 1-8 weeks, the setup is more fragile than at prior review: (a) price has consolidated flat near 52W highs, suggesting the easy re-rating is done; (b) cotton cost reversal creates margin headwind that wasn't present earlier; (c) Q4 FY26 results (expected May 2026) are the key binary catalyst — strong results could push through Rs 404 resistance, disappointing results could trigger profit-taking from Rs 386-392 levels. The 1-8 week window sits squarely on this event. Analyst targets (Rs 453-471) still imply upside, but consensus already factors in the trade deals. Net: more event-driven than momentum-driven for the next 1-8 weeks.

**Contradictions, Weaknesses, And Open Risks:** (1) Cotton price reversal — Shankar 6 at Rs 60,500/candy (+8.5% MoM) with structural supply deficit is a direct margin risk, especially if Q4 FY26 or Q1 FY27 results show margin compression; (2) Q3 FY26 profit fell 5% despite 14% revenue growth — operating leverage not materializing as expected; (3) India's total textile exports grew only 2.1% in FY 2025-26, suggesting FTA benefits are still early-stage at sector level; (4) Five-year revenue CAGR 2.48% and ROE 10.1% mean structural re-rating requires sustained multi-quarter execution that history doesn't support; (5) Promoter holding ~39.5% — manageable but not strong alignment; (6) US consumer demand post-tariff disruption uncertain — even with favourable tariff math, volumes depend on end-demand; (7) Relief rally characterization by market strategists suggests current tariff advantage may not sustain prolonged upside.

**Peer Placement Notes:** Ranks in middle-high tier among Indian textile exporters benefiting from FTA/tariff catalysts. Scale (Rs 8,390 Cr revenue), vertical integration (denim + wovens + garments), and US+EU dual exposure are genuine differentiators vs smaller domestic-only peers. However, cotton sensitivity, mixed earnings quality, and low return ratios place it below higher-ROCE exporters or technical textile players with less input-cost vulnerability. The flat price consolidation and cotton cost reversal since Apr 19 slightly weaken its relative positioning vs peers that may have fresher price momentum or better margin trajectories. Within the 1-8 week swing frame, the Q4 FY26 result event makes this more of a catalyst-dependent position than a momentum position.
