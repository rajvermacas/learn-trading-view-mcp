### Sponsorship Reasoning

#### 1. Recent Trigger — What Likely Drove The Move

Since the prior review (19 Apr 2026), CG Power rallied 6.25% in the week of 20-24 Apr, hitting a new 52-week high of Rs 846.9 on 24 Apr 2026. The move is multi-factor: (a) anticipation of Q4 FY26 results scheduled for 6 May 2026, with Q4 being seasonally the strongest quarter; (b) CG Semi OSAT G1 facility reportedly beginning commercial production (as of early March 2026, production was expected within six weeks); (c) continued re-rating of Indian industrial/power capex names amid strong domestic capex cycle; (d) the Rs 900 Cr Tallgrass data-center transformer order (Jan 2026) providing global hyperscale data-center exposure narrative. No single event trigger identified for the week — this is a multi-catalyst convergence at a pre-results window.

#### 2. Operating Evidence — Latest Quarterly Data And OPM Trend

Q3 FY26 (Dec 2025) consolidated: Revenue Rs 3,175 Cr (+26.2% YoY, +8.6% QoQ). Operating profit Rs 362 Cr (-4.0% QoQ despite revenue growth). OPM 11.39% — down from 12.89% (Q2 FY26), 13.25% (Q1 FY26), and 13.16% (Q3 FY25). PAT Rs 285 Cr (+18.4% YoY, -0.7% QoQ). The OPM compression is now a clear two-quarter trend, falling 186 bps from Q1 to Q3 FY26, signaling commodity cost absorption or product-mix shift that is not being offset by operating leverage. Revenue growth remains robust at 26% YoY, but the disconnect between top-line growth and bottom-line delivery is widening.

Quarterly revenue progression (consolidated): Rs 2,516 Cr (Q3 FY25) to Rs 2,753 Cr (Q4 FY25) to Rs 2,878 Cr (Q1 FY26) to Rs 2,923 Cr (Q2 FY26) to Rs 3,175 Cr (Q3 FY26). The sequential acceleration in Q3 (8.6% QoQ vs 1.5% in Q2) is encouraging for revenue momentum.

#### 3. Earnings Quality — PAT Quality Without One-Offs

TTM PAT approximately Rs 1,074-1,109 Cr (derived from Q4 FY25 through Q3 FY26). This is operationally clean — the FY24 distortion from Rs 684 Cr Other Income is fully lapped. Tax rate normalized at 26-28%. No significant one-off items detected in recent quarters. EPS on TTM basis approximately Rs 7.0-7.3. Earnings quality is now plain and transparent, but the absolute level of earnings is thin relative to the 116x multiple. For context, at P/E 116x and TTM EPS Rs 7.1, the stock is pricing in forward EPS of approximately Rs 12-15 within 2-3 years (assuming de-rating to 60-70x), which requires 70-110% PAT growth — far above the current 18% YoY trajectory.

#### 4. Balance Sheet Comfort

Near-zero debt (D/E ~0.01, borrowings Rs 41 Cr against equity Rs 4,069 Cr). ROE 27.7%, ROCE 37.5% — high-quality return metrics. Operating cash flow Rs 944-1,028 Cr annually, FCF Rs 519 Cr (FY25). The CG Semi OSAT investment (Rs 7,600 Cr over 5 years) is housed in a separate JV entity with Renesas and Stars Microelectronics, supported by government ISM subsidy, and does not directly encumber the parent balance sheet. The Indonesian subsidiary divestment (completed 1 Apr 2026) had nil financial impact. Income tax revisionary order (Rs 21.4 Cr disallowance for AY 2022-23) is immaterial and under appeal. No balance sheet fragility whatsoever.

#### 5. Catalyst Status — KAVACH, Semiconductor/OSAT, Tallgrass, Axiro

**KAVACH**: Mixed status. The original Rs 433 Cr KAVACH order (Nov 2024) from Chittaranjan Locomotive Works had its Loco KAVACH component (Rs 500-600 Cr) cancelled in Nov 2025 because GG Tronics could not start deliveries within the required timeline due to pending RDSO and ISA approvals. GG Tronics remains qualified for future tenders under the Developmental Category and is in advanced stages of securing KAVACH 4.0 approval. The braking algorithm development is progressing. Revenue impact is delayed, not eliminated.

**Semiconductor OSAT**: The most significant development since the prior review. CG Semi G1 facility (0.5M units/day peak capacity) was reported in early March 2026 to begin commercial production within six weeks, implying production may have commenced by mid-April 2026. G2 facility (14.5M units/day capacity) is under construction, expected completion by end-CY2026. This is the first time the OSAT thesis is transitioning from narrative to operational reality. However, G1 is a small-scale initial ramp — material revenue contribution to CG Power's consolidated P&L is unlikely before FY27-FY28.

**Tallgrass Data Center Order**: Rs 900 Cr order (Jan 2026) from Tallgrass Integrated Logistics Solutions, USA, for power transformers for hyperscale data center. Largest single order ever won by CG Power. 12-20 month delivery timeline. This opens a new global vertical (data center infrastructure) and validates CG Power's transformer capability for mission-critical applications.

**Axiro/EdgeCortix**: Axiro Semiconductor (CG Power group) partnered with EdgeCortix for energy-efficient AI processing at the edge (Apr 2026). Early-stage investment providing semiconductor narrative depth but not revenue-contributing in the near term.

#### 6. Evidence-to-Price Alignment

At CMP Rs 823, market cap Rs 1,29,641 Cr, P/E ~116x, P/B 17.4x. The stock prices in a semiconductor-level growth story on top of industrial revenue growth. Current evidence supports 25-26% revenue CAGR but only 15-18% PAT growth due to OPM compression. The multiple demands 30-40% earnings CAGR for 3-5 years. The gap between what the price demands and what current financials deliver remains the primary risk. The OSAT production start and Tallgrass order improve the forward evidence base, but neither is reflected in reported earnings yet. Analyst consensus target is Rs 790 (mean of 16 analysts), with range Rs 574-940 — current price of Rs 823 is above the mean target, suggesting the market is ahead of consensus estimates.

#### 7. Event-Risk Modifier — Q4 FY26 Results Timing

**Critical near-term event**: Board meeting on 6 May 2026 to approve Q4 FY26 and full-year FY26 audited results. Trading window closed until 8 May 2026. This is the single most important event for the next 1-8 week window.

**Scenarios**: If Q4 FY26 (seasonally strongest) delivers revenue Rs 3,400-3,600 Cr and OPM recovers to 12.5-13%+, PAT could reach Rs 320-370 Cr, supporting the momentum. If OPM stays at 11% or compresses further, PAT could disappoint at Rs 270-300 Cr, making the 116x multiple indefensible and triggering profit-booking from the 52-week high. Any commentary on OSAT revenue timeline or new order wins would be incremental catalysts.

**FII selling**: FII stake at 12.03% (Mar 2026), down from 15.39% (Mar 2023) — three consecutive years of institutional selling. This structural headwind limits sustained re-rating unless reversed by OSAT/data-center narrative attracting new institutional flows.
