## Sponsorship Reasoning

**Why Sponsorship Is Present Or Not:** Present because SG Finserve delivered genuinely extraordinary operating year in FY26. Revenue doubled (₹170 Cr FY25 → ₹333.7 Cr FY26), PAT grew 58% (₹80.99 Cr → ₹127.66 Cr), loan book expanded 75% to ₹3,936 Cr. Not marginal improvements — business that has successfully crossed ₹2,000 Cr AUM threshold where supply chain NBFCs typically demonstrate operating leverage. Short-cycle (30-35 day) nature of receivables means book quality transparent near-real-time. AA/A1+ credit ratings from two agencies (CRISIL and ICRA) confirm balance sheet soundness. Not "strong" because ROE trajectory still developing (12% delivered vs 18-20% targeted), asset quality self-reported rather than independently disclosed, stock already recovered substantially from lows (₹308 52-week low to ₹538 52-week high, CMP ₹513.65).

**Recent Trigger And What Likely Drove The Move:** 3M momentum has two legs. First, Madhusudan Kela's March 2025 bulk buy at ₹350 triggered market awareness of SG Finserve as undiscovered supply chain NBFC backed by APL Apollo group with strong operating metrics. Ashish Kacholia's subsequent doubling of stake to 2.37% by March 2026 reinforced smart-money signal. Second, FY26 operating delivery validated thesis: Q3 FY26 (January 2026) showed revenue +103% YoY and PAT +37% YoY; Q4 FY26 (April 16, 2026 earnings call) showed revenue +95% YoY and PAT +78% YoY. April 2026 earnings call revealed ₹3,936 Cr loan book and confirmed ₹6,000 Cr FY27 target, directly preceding 12.94% 1-week return and 37.44% 1-month return. Price action reflects investor base catching up to business executing well in relative obscurity.

**Business And Financial Evidence:** Loan book history: FY23 ₹975 Cr → FY24 ~₹1,500 Cr → FY25 ₹2,246 Cr → Q1 FY26 ₹2,504 Cr → Q2 FY26 ₹2,878 Cr → Q4 FY26 ₹3,936 Cr. 5-year revenue CAGR 200.7%.
