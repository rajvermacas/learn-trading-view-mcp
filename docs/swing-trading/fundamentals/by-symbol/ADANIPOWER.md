### Sponsorship Reasoning

- **Why Sponsorship Is Present Or Not**: Sponsorship is present, primarily driven by structural capacity expansion and sentiment-level catalysts rather than confirmed near-term earnings acceleration. The business generates Rs 21,501 Cr in CFO annually, holds 90% PPA coverage on 18,150 MW, and is executing a 23.7 GW expansion with 11.7 GW already under PPA — these are concrete business facts that underpin the stock's re-rating. The Hindenburg shutdown and SEC dismissal motion removed two of the most significant overhangs on the Adani group, allowing fundamentals to re-price without the discount. However, TTM earnings are in a genuine trough (-14% to -19% YoY) due to subdued power demand and merchant tariff compression, and the stock is already at all-time highs after a 41% run. Sponsorship is real but the easy money from the re-rating may already be made.

- **Recent Trigger And What Likely Drove The Move**: The 3-month 41.22% rally (approximately January–April 2026) was driven by three stacked events. First, the Hindenburg Research shutdown (early 2025 / confirmed fully in 2025) progressively removed the group-level overhang — the Jan 1, 2026 session saw Adani Power jump 7.15% alone on New Year momentum. Second, the US court acceptance on April 7, 2026 of Adani's request for a pre-motion conference on dismissing the SEC fraud case triggered the most recent leg — the stock hit all-time highs on April 15–17, 2026, gaining 21.5% in nine sessions with a 5.8% after-hours surge on April 16. Third, the Q3 FY26 Assam PPA (3,200 MW at Rs 6.30/unit energy charge) demonstrated that capacity absorption by state utilities is proceeding at scale, validating the long-term growth thesis. The YoY declines in revenue and profit were market-known before the rally — the price move was sentiment and catalyst driven, not earnings driven.

- **Business And Financial Evidence**: Full-year FY25: Revenue Rs 56,203 Cr (5-year CAGR 16%), NP Rs 12,750 Cr (5-year CAGR 66%), OPM 38%, CFO Rs 21,501 Cr, FCF Rs 9,957 Cr. FY24 NP Rs 20,829 Cr was peak (exceptional tariff conditions) — FY25 NP represents normalization. Q3 FY26 (Dec 2025 quarter): Revenue Rs 12,717 Cr (-5.3% YoY), EBITDA Rs 4,636 Cr (-3.1% YoY), PAT Rs 2,488 Cr (-15.4% YoY). PLF 62.6% vs 63.9% — stable utilization on flat demand. Merchant tariff Rs 3.22/kWh vs Rs 3.71 in Q3 FY25 (-13.2%). 9M FY26: Revenue Rs 40,524 Cr, PAT Rs 8,700 Cr. Annual capacity: 18,150 MW installed, 90% under PPA. Borrowings: Rs 39,495 Cr (FY25). Equity + Reserves: ~Rs 56,347 Cr.

- **Catalyst Context**: (1) Hindenburg Research shutdown: The short-seller behind the $100 billion Adani rout (January 2023) disbanded, removing the most persistent source of selling pressure on Adani group. This is a permanent event, not a temporary sentiment shift. (2) SEC case dismissal motion (April 7–30, 2026): US court accepted pre-motion conference request; formal dismissal motion due by April 30. Adani's legal argument centers on Indian jurisdiction — if accepted, it eliminates the most prominent active legal risk. If rejected, a sharp group-level sell-off is possible. (3) Assam DISCOM 3,200 MW PPA at Rs 6.30/unit energy charge: DBFOO structure (Design-Build-Finance-Own-Operate) — long-term revenue-visible contract at a healthy tariff. This brings total expansion PPA tie-ups to 11.7 GW of the 23.7 GW pipeline. (4) Mahan Phase-II (1,600 MW) at 80% completion: imminent capacity addition that will directly uplift revenue by an estimated Rs 3,000-4,000 Cr annually when fully operational under its existing PPA. (5) Q4 FY26 results board meeting April 29, 2026: Q4 is typically summer peak demand (highest PLF quarter). Q4 FY25 would have been materially stronger than Q3 FY25, and if Q4 FY26 follows the same seasonal pattern, the trend of YoY NP decline could inflect.

- **Why The Rally Looks Legit Or Fragile For 1-8 Weeks**: Legitimate aspects: the Hindenburg removal and SEC dismissal motion are real events that changed the risk calculus for institutional allocation to Adani group. The capacity expansion with 90% PPA coverage means revenue visibility is high — this is not a merchant-tariff speculation play. Q4 FY26 results (April 29) provide a near-term catalyst within the window; Q4 is historically the strongest quarter for Indian power companies (summer peak). The AA credit ratings on Rs 69,000 Cr of facilities provide institutional anchoring. Fragile aspects: at all-time highs after a 41% run, the stock has priced in significant optionality. The SEC case is not resolved — it is at the pre-motion conference stage, and a formal dismissal is not guaranteed. Q3 FY26 results were genuinely weak (PAT -15% YoY), and if Q4 also disappoints, the narrative of "temporary trough" breaks. Merchant tariff at Rs 3.22/kWh is meaningfully below FY25 levels and this compression may not be purely seasonal. The gap between FY24 peak NP (Rs 20,829 Cr) and current run-rate suggests normalization is ongoing, and P/E 33x on trough earnings is a pricing that leaves limited room for further disappointment.

- **Contradictions, Weaknesses, And Open Risks**:
  1. FY24 NP (Rs 20,829 Cr) to FY25 NP (Rs 12,750 Cr) is a 39% decline — this is not a small trough; it is a structural reset of profitability post-FY24 peak tariff conditions. The stock is priced at 33x P/E on an earnings base that is already 39% below its own peak from the prior year.
  2. SEC bribery case is unresolved. The April 7 court action was an acceptance of a pre-motion conference, not a dismissal. The case involves alleged payment of hundreds of millions in bribes to Indian government officials — if the dismissal motion fails, the legal overhang returns fully.
  3. Merchant tariff compression is secular: Rs 3.22/kWh in Q3 FY26 vs Rs 3.71 in Q3 FY25 — as renewable capacity grows in India, merchant power prices are structurally under pressure. Adani Power's strategy to reduce merchant exposure from 20% to 10% of volumes is prudent but takes time and compresses near-term merchant income.
  4. Heavy leverage (Rs 39,495 Cr borrowings) combined with a Rs 7,500 Cr NCD raise in January 2026 means balance sheet expansion is ongoing. The capex to build 23.7 GW of new capacity will further increase debt before new capacity revenues come online.
  5. Power demand growth in India was only 0.5% in 9M FY26 — structurally, India's power demand is expected to grow 6-8% annually, but near-term softness creates downside risk to PLF and merchant volumes.
  6. At all-time highs (Rs 193.37 on April 15), the stock is technically extended. A 10-15% correction from all-time highs, even without a fundamental change, is consistent with profit-taking behavior in high-momentum stocks.
  7. The 3-year profit CAGR of 38% and 5-year profit CAGR of 66% are backward-looking figures that reflect exceptional FY23 and FY24 earnings conditions — forward earnings growth will be much more moderate as the base normalizes.

- **Peer Placement Notes**: Within a momentum screen universe, ADANIPOWER occupies the category of "large-cap re-rating on sentiment reversal plus structural capacity growth." The 41.22% 3-month return is one of the strongest in the universe, supported by multiple catalysts. It should rank above peers with weaker momentum, lower PPA coverage, or no near-term catalyst within the 8-week window. However, it should rank below any peer where earnings are actively growing (not in a trough), where P/E is lower on current numbers, and where the legal/regulatory overhang is absent. Within the power sector, ADANIPOWER's scale (18,150 MW), PPA coverage (90%), and expansion pipeline (23.7 GW) are without parallel — but this scale premium is already embedded in the 33x P/E. For a swing trading context, the key risk is the stock has already made its largest move and is at all-time highs with results due in 10 days — the asymmetry for the next 8 weeks is meaningfully less favorable than it was 3 months ago.
