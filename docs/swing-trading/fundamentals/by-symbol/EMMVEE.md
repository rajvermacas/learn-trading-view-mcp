**Comparison Dimensions**:

- **Recent Trigger**: Credible, but largely IPO-era re-rating. Q3 FY26 result showed revenue +118% YoY and PAT +166% YoY. However, standalone data shows Dec 2025 standalone revenue of only ₹81 Cr with -₹7 Cr operating loss — consolidated growth driven substantially by subsidiary EEPL.
- **Operating Evidence**: Consolidated is genuine, standalone is erratic. Consolidated quarterly trajectory clean: ₹402 Cr (Sep 2024) → ₹1,152 Cr (Dec 2025). OPM stable at 34-38%. 9M FY26 revenue up 162%, PAT up 326%. 10.3 GW module capacity, order book of 9.3 GW.
- **Earnings Quality**: Capacity-driven, some one-off risk at standalone. OPM expanded from 13% in FY24 to 31% in FY25, running at 34-36% in FY26 quarters. ROCE 28-36.5%. However, 55% of raw material from China — supply chain risk. Inventory Days at 197, CCC at 136 days.
- **Balance Sheet Comfort**: Post-IPO deleveraged but capex cycle continues. Pre-IPO borrowings ₹2,065 Cr. IPO raised ₹2,900 Cr; ~65% for debt repayment. Net D/E fell to -0.02x. However, 51% of EEPL subsidiary shares pledged against ₹1,897 Cr IREDA loan. Phase 2 capex will reload balance sheet.
- **Catalyst Credibility**: Strong and visible. (1) 40% BCD on imported modules; (2) ALMM List-II mandatory from June 2026; (3) PLI scheme ₹24,000 Cr; (4) 9.3 GW order book with 6.3 GW deliverable in 12-18 months; (5) 4.5 GW multi-year TOPCon cell contract through 2030.
- **Evidence-to-Price Alignment**: Not stretched. Consolidated TTM P/E ~33.9x. Forward P/E approximately 19-20x. Peer sector P/E averaging 47x.
- **Near-Term Fragility**: Several real risks. (1) Q4 FY26 results pending; (2) March 2026 customs penalty ₹7.68 Cr being appealed; (3) Customer concentration: top customer 36.6% of revenue; (4) EEPL subsidiary pledge; (5) China raw material dependency 55%.

**Ranking Summary**:

- **Why It Ranks Here**: Genuine, verifiable, large-magnitude earnings growth driven by capacity expansion and sector tailwinds. Order book is real (9.3 GW), ROCE among highest in solar peer set, policy support is structural.
- **What Keeps It From Ranking Higher**: (1) Standalone financials erratic and declining; (2) 51% EEPL shares pledged to IREDA; (3) Heavy customer concentration; (4) Massive inventory days and long CCC; (5) Free cash flow deeply negative.
- **What Kind Of Peer Should Rank Above It**: A peer with no subsidiary pledge risk, positive free cash flow, lower customer concentration, more diversified raw material sourcing.
- **What Kind Of Peer Should Rank Below It**: Peers showing 3-month price moves without corresponding earnings delivery, ROCE below 15%, or speculative order books.
