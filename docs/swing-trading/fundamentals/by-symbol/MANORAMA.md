### Comparison Dimensions

**Recent Trigger — Credible structural demand shift, partially confirmed**
Global cocoa shortage (2023-2025) elevated demand for cocoa butter equivalents (CBE). Manorama is a global CBE manufacturer using sal, mango, and shea seeds. Care Edge upgraded to A+/A1+ on March 27, 2026 — hard third-party confirmation. The 3-month move partially reflects recovery from correction, not just new catalyst entry.

**Operating Evidence — Multi-quarter acceleration confirmed**
Sales: Rs 209 Cr (Dec 2024) → Rs 363 Cr (Dec 2025) — 73% jump. Net profit: Rs 30 Cr → Rs 68 Cr — 131% jump. OPM held at 27% across five consecutive quarters — structural margin re-rating. TTM 9-month revenues Rs 975 Cr, EBITDA Rs 265 Cr, PAT Rs 174 Cr.

**Earnings Quality — Weak point; CFO significant concern**
FY25 CFO negative at -Rs 59 Cr despite PAT Rs 112 Cr. Negative in 3 of 4 years. Debtor days rose from 33 to 48 days. Inventory days 516 — extremely elevated but consistent with exotic seed-based raw materials. Volatile negative CFO is the single most important red flag.

**Balance Sheet Comfort — Moderate; D/E elevated but rated**
Borrowings Rs 482 Cr against equity ~Rs 462 Cr gives D/E 1.07x. Elevated for company with negative CFO. Care Edge A+ LT rating provides external comfort. Rs 460 Cr capex ahead will likely increase borrowings. Working capital days 105, inventory 516 days.

**Catalyst Credibility — Confirmed and multi-year**
Three catalysts: (1) Global cocoa shortage driving structural CBE demand; (2) Global market share growing from 2% (FY25) to 5% (FY27) with 75,000 MTPA capacity expansion; (3) Care Edge A+/A1+ upgrade March 2026.

**Evidence-to-Price Alignment — Stretched but not absurd**
CMP Rs 1,447, P/E ~40x trailing. TTM EPS ~Rs 29 implies P/E ~50x on actual TTM. P/B 15x on D/E 1.07x with inconsistent CFO. Valuation embeds significant execution. 52-week high Rs 1,774 — stock 18% below high.

**Near-Term Fragility — Moderate**
Q4 FY26 results likely May 2026 — seasonal weakness possible. Cocoa price normalization risk. Rs 460 Cr capex at D/E 1.07x. Promoter stake declined from 57.26% to 54.32% over 6 quarters.

### Ranking Summary

## Sponsorship Reasoning

### Why Sponsorship Is Present Or Not
Present but not decisive. Business case is real — genuine beneficiary of global structural cocoa butter sourcing shift. Quarterly numbers consistently improving 5+ quarters. Credit upgrade is institutional validation. Cash flow undermines strength — 131% YoY profit growth should convert to operating cash flow, and consistently negative CFO is a yellow flag.

### Recent Trigger And What Likely Drove The Move
Dec 2025 quarterly results (Rs 363 Cr revenue, Rs 68 Cr PAT) above consensus plus continued cocoa scarcity narrative. The 3-month period likely captures recovery from mid-range pullback, not fresh breakout — stock still 18% below 52-week high.

### Business And Financial Evidence
- Revenue quarterly: Rs 209 → 233 → 290 → 323 → 363 Cr — uninterrupted acceleration
- Profit: Rs 30 → 42 → 51 → 55 → 68 Cr — same acceleration
- OPM 27% held across 5 quarters — new structural floor
- Annual: Revenue Rs 457 Cr (FY24) → Rs 771 Cr (FY25), profit Rs 40 Cr → Rs 112 Cr
- 5-year revenue CAGR 33%, profit CAGR 37%
- TTM 9-month PAT Rs 174 Cr vs full-year FY25 Rs 112 Cr — on track for Rs 220-230 Cr FY26
- ROCE 23%, ROE 28%
- CFO: Negative in 3 of 4 years

### Catalyst Context
Global cocoa shortage is structural and multi-year. Chocolate majors (Ferrero, Mars, Nestlé, Mondelez) diversifying from pure cocoa butter. Manorama ranked 5th globally in CBE. Growing from 2% to 5-6% global share over 3 years with Rs 460 Cr capacity expansion. Care Edge A+/A1+ upgrade signals institutional lender confidence.

### Why The Rally Looks Legit Or Fragile For 1-8 Weeks
Legit: Multi-quarter strong numbers, recent credit upgrade, no promoter pledge, CBE demand theme intact, 18% below high. Fragile: Q4 FY26 may show seasonal weakness, cocoa price normalization risk, 40x P/E zero tolerance for misses, negative CFO could concern analysts.

### Contradictions, Weaknesses, And Open Risks
1. CFO/PAT ratio: Rs 112 Cr PAT but -Rs 59 Cr CFO in FY25, negative 3 of 4 years
2. Promoter stake declined 57.26% to 54.32% over 6 quarters without disclosed reason
3. D/E 1.07x + Rs 460 Cr capex could push to 1.5-2.0x
4. Inventory 516 days — monsoon/forest access disruption risk
5. P/E ~40-50x limits margin of safety

### Peer Placement Notes
Middle tier among momentum stocks with strong quarterly numbers. Ranks above any stock with momentum but no multi-quarter confirmation or ROCE <15%. Ranks below stocks with same growth but positive CFO, lower D/E, and P/E <30x.
