Sponsorship is earned, not speculative.

Together, these three triggers span the entire 3-month window and explain the sustained (not one-day) nature of the rally.

- **Contradictions, Weaknesses, And Open Risks**:
  1. Negative operating leverage in 9M FY26 — revenue grew 19% but PAT grew only 9%. This is concerning at a 56x P/E; valuation requires operating leverage to materialize in H2 FY27 as 31 awaiting programs ramp.
  2. EBITDA margin guidance revised downward to 24-26% (from 25-27%) post railway acquisition — structural margin compression, not a temporary phenomenon. Each incremental India/non-auto revenue unit is dilutive to group margins.
  3. BYD deal cancellation — "significant business development setback" per management, with no quantified revenue impact disclosed. At 56x P/E, any meaningful customer concentration loss is a multiple de-rating risk.
  4. China rare earth supply restrictions — company has shifted to light rare earth designs as mitigation, but this is a permanent cost and complexity headwind for its motor products.
  5. North America EV volume decline 36-45% sequentially — while geographic diversification is working as a mitigation, the US EV market recovery timing is unknown. Any sustained US EV slowdown limits the higher-margin program ramp that justifies the ₹6,500 Cr FY27 target.
  6. Promoter holding declined 25.5% over three years — continuous dilution for growth funding is a shareholder concern; if growth doesn't materialize at the expected pace, the stock will face multiple compression.
  7. Capex-heavy growth mode — ₹562 Cr capex in FY25 on ₹775 Cr CFO means FCF yield is modest (~1%) at current market cap. The company is investing aggressively, which is right for its order book, but creates execution dependency.
  8. Q4 FY26 results within the 8-week swing window creates a binary event risk — the stock may have already partially priced in a strong Q4; any quarter-on-quarter revenue decline from Q3's record ₹1,209 Cr risks being interpreted as deceleration.

It sits in the "compounders" tier — stocks where the market is already paying for multi-year earnings growth.
