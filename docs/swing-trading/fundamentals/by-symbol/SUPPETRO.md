## Sponsorship Reasoning

### Why Sponsorship Is Present Or Not

Sponsorship is mixed and leans toward the weaker end of mixed. Business quality fundamentals are real: 50% domestic polystyrene market share, sole XPS producer in India, near-zero debt, CRISIL AA, self-funded capex — these are the hallmarks of a durable franchise. But the financial trajectory in FY26 has been uniformly negative: three consecutive quarters of OPM compression (8% → 7% → 6%), NP declining each quarter, and 9M FY26 cumulative NP ₹159 Cr vs ₹284 Cr in the same period FY25. The 45.49% price rally occurred *after* Q3 FY26 results (Jan 23, 2026) revealed a -57.7% NP collapse — meaning sponsorship is entirely forward-looking. The market has pre-priced recovery from: (a) ABS plant restoration, (b) EPS Phase II commissioning, and (c) styrene spread normalization. None of these recoveries have appeared in a published quarter. Sponsorship is present as a thesis but absent as confirmed evidence.

### Recent Trigger And What Likely Drove The Move

The price rally from approximately ₹534 (52-week low zone) to ₹775 CMP over 3 months (Jan–Apr 2026) is best explained by three overlapping catalysts:

1. **ABS plant restart narrative**: The mass ABS plant at Amdoshi, Nagothane failed in December 2025 (coinciding with Q3 FY26 results), disrupting a brand-new 70,000 TPA plant. Repair work with Versatis Italy concluded in March 2026, with plant resuming at 65% capacity. The market re-rated the stock upward as the technical disruption risk was removed.

2. **EPS Phase II capacity expansion**: On April 14, 2026 — just five days before this analysis — Supreme Petrochem announced commissioning of EPS Phase II at Amdoshi, adding 30,000 TPA (35% capacity increase) for ₹54 Cr from internal accruals. This is a confirmed operational event that validates growth execution capability.

3. **Anticipatory Q4 FY26 buy**: Board meeting April 24, 2026 to consider Q4 results and final dividend. Market participants buying ahead of this, expecting Q4 to show margin recovery as styrene prices stabilized and ABS resumed partial operations. Volume inquiry on March 30, 2026 by the exchange (company confirmed no undisclosed event) suggests elevated market speculation in late March.

The rally is not sponsored by confirmed earnings improvement. It is sponsored by removal of a negative (ABS plant fixed), addition of a positive (EPS capacity), and forward anticipation of results in 5 days.

### Contradictions, Weaknesses, And Open Risks

1. **Price-earnings paradox**: Stock is at P/E 241 (screener TTM consolidated), down 45% from 52-week high of ₹982 and up 45% over 3 months from lows — it has simultaneously been both a 48% drawdown and a 45% recovery. This indicates extreme sentiment swings, not steady accumulation by informed investors.

2. **Declining TTM earnings trend**: Three consecutive quarters of declining NP — ₹82 Cr, ₹48 Cr, ₹30 Cr. This is a deteriorating trend, not a one-quarter dip. For the Q4 recovery thesis to work, Q4 would need to deliver approximately ₹80-100 Cr NP to bring the annual total to ₹260-370 Cr — achievable but requiring a dramatic single-quarter reversal.

3. **CRISIL outlook cut contradicts recovery pricing**: Rating agencies lag markets but CRISIL's February 2026 outlook revision to Stable signals that even a conservative credit analyst did not see sufficient evidence of near-term margin restoration to maintain a Positive outlook.

4. **ABS plant international dependency**: Equipment failure requiring Versatis Italy technical support is a quality signal concern — the plant suffered a significant failure within its first operational year. Restart at 65% capacity, not 100%, suggests residual risk.

5. **Cash reserves at lowest recent level (₹342 Cr)**: Against ₹250-275 Cr committed for next EPS capex (Q1 FY27) and ongoing working capital for the new plants, the cash buffer is tightening despite no debt. Not a crisis but leaves less margin for error.

6. **Styrene price sensitivity**: Revenue is directly linked to styrene monomer pricing. At $810/tonne, spreads are compressed. Any further drop (crude oil weakness, new global PS capacity addition from China) extends the earnings depression.

7. **Global trade uncertainty (April 2026)**: US tariff environment in April 2026 is creating demand uncertainty for polystyrene end-uses in electronics, packaging, and automotive — Supreme's ABS plant is exposed to discretionary demand cycles.

8. **No promoter buying to signal conviction**: Promoter holding stable at 64.24% — neither buying nor selling. In a recovery setup, promoter buying on the open market would be a conviction signal; its absence is neutral but not positive.
