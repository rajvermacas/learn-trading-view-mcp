The 30%+ 3-month return is anchored in a specific price-history narrative: the stock bottomed at ₹2,115 on January 23, 2026 after a HVDC order-loss-driven derating in December 2025, then recovered sharply on Q3 FY26 earnings strength (PAT +35% YoY, revenue +26% YoY) and sector re-rating. **Comparison Dimensions**:

- **Recent Trigger**: Credible recovery — The 30%+ move is a recovery from a temporary December 2025 derating caused by a single HVDC order loss to GE Vernova. The stock fell from ~₹3,100 to ₹2,115 (ATL). The rebound to ~₹3,020 was powered by Q3 FY26 results (PAT ₹313 Cr, +35% YoY; revenue ₹1,911 Cr, +26% YoY) and broad power sector re-rating. The trigger is real and verifiable, not pure sentiment.

- **Operating Evidence**: Strong with a margin-expansion story — OPM expanded from 14% (Jun 2024) to 24% (Sep 2025 and Dec 2025), a 10-percentage-point improvement over 6 quarters. Revenue trajectory: Jun 2024 ₹1,484 Cr → Dec 2024 ₹1,517 Cr → Mar 2025 ₹1,880 Cr → Jun 2025 ₹1,785 Cr → Sep 2025 ₹2,646 Cr → Dec 2025 ₹1,911 Cr. Sequential step-up is visible. Power Transmission (55% of revenue) delivered 35% YoY revenue growth in Q3 with EBIT margin of 18.7%, up 860 bps YoY. The operating story is not superficial.

No red flags on PAT composition.

- **Balance Sheet Comfort**: Strong — Borrowings of only ₹148 Cr against reserves of ₹4,310 Cr. Effectively debt-free. Free cash flow of ₹3,454 Cr (TTM). Balance sheet is not a constraint on growth and provides significant cushion against execution surprises. No leverage risk in the 1-8 week window.

- **Catalyst Credibility**: Real but partially consumed — The order book of ₹16,205 Cr as of Sep 2025 (47% YoY growth) provides multi-quarter revenue visibility at roughly 2x annualized revenue. Nine-month FY25 order inflows were ₹107.7 Bn. The ongoing ₹740 Cr capex to double transformer capacity and expand GIS manufacturing is a structural catalyst. The risk: HVDC is lumpy and the December 2025 loss to GE Vernova showed competitive pressure exists. Order inflow normalization on a high FY25 base is a stated risk.

- **Evidence-to-Price Alignment**: Stretched but not irrational — At P/E 88x on TTM EPS of ₹30.89, and CMP ~₹3,020, the stock is pricing significant forward growth. Analysts value it at 60x Sep 2027E EPS, implying a FY27E P/E of ~54x. At 30% CAGR revenue projection and 37% PAT CAGR through FY27, the multiple is defensible in a sector-hot environment, but there is little room for earnings disappointment. The stock is priced for execution perfection, which caps the upside for a swing trade entry here.

- **Near-Term Fragility**: Present — Three specific risks over the next 8 weeks: (1) Multiple senior management exits (Execution Unit Heads for Grid Technologies Transformer and Competence Hub, plus Finance Head for Grid Solutions) effective March-April 2026, creating interim coverage gaps in key execution roles; (2) No Q4 FY26 results announcement before May 10, so no near-term earnings catalyst to sustain momentum; (3) HVDC order flow is lumpy — any missed HVDC award in Apr-May 2026 would reprise the December 2025 derating pattern. Downside to ₹2,600-₹2,700 remains visible if any of these materialize.
