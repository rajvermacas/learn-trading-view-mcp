### Sponsorship Reasoning

- **Why Sponsorship Is Present Or Not**: Sponsorship is present but conditional. The business evidence is genuine — Q3 FY26 PAT surged 80% to Rs 205 Cr (though Rs 59 Cr of this is exceptional gains), order backlog is Rs 12,641 Cr, and the company won a Rs 1,600 Cr boiler order and Rs 200 Cr in data center cooling orders. The green hydrogen electrolyzer ramp to mid-2026 production and the Exactspace AI acquisition add credibility to the energy transition narrative. However, sponsorship is conditional because: (1) OPM has been structurally capped at 7-10% for five years; (2) core underlying Q3 PAT, ex-exceptionals, is Rs 145-150 Cr — the 80% headline jump is partly optical; (3) at 77x P/E, any stumble in Q4 FY26 results destroys momentum sharply; and (4) FII selling over two consecutive quarters suggests smart money is trimming at these levels.

- **Recent Trigger And What Likely Drove The Move**: The 40.97% 3-month rally (Jan–Apr 2026) was driven by a layered sequence, not a single catalyst. The first wave came from the Q3 FY26 results (Feb 2, 2026) when PAT surged 80% and order intake jumped 34% — stock gained ~10% on the day. The second wave was the Rs 1,600 Cr TBWES boiler order announcement (March 27, 2026), which validated Thermax's positioning in the ultra-supercritical coal thermal power capex cycle. The third wave was the Exactspace AI acquisition (April 9, 2026) — small at Rs 30.48 Cr but carrying an AI narrative premium that drove another ~10% weekly gain. The underlying macro tailwind was the India capex supercycle narrative (industrial, green energy, thermal power) re-rating capital goods companies broadly from Jan 2026 onwards.

- **Business And Financial Evidence**: Consolidated financial data (FY25): Sales Rs 10,389 Cr (vs Rs 9,323 Cr FY24, +11.4% YoY), Net Profit Rs 627 Cr (vs Rs 643 Cr FY24, -2.5% YoY), OPM 9%, ROCE 16%, ROE 14%. Quarterly trajectory FY26: Q1 Rs 2,158 Cr / OPM 10% / NP Rs 151 Cr; Q2 Rs 2,474 Cr / OPM 7% / NP Rs 119 Cr; Q3 Rs 2,635 Cr / OPM 10% / NP Rs 205 Cr (including Rs 59 Cr exceptional). Order backlog Rs 12,641 Cr (Dec 31, 2025). CFO FY25 Rs 1,043 Cr, FCF Rs 143 Cr (thin), D/E 0.35 (rising). FII shareholding 11.25% (declined 366 bps over two quarters). Promoter holding 61.99% (stable). 5-year sales CAGR 13%, 5-year profit CAGR 25%, 10-year stock CAGR 18%.

- **Catalyst Context**: (1) TBWES Rs 1,600 Cr boiler order for 800 MW ultra-supercritical thermal power project (Central India, signed March 27, 2026) — adds directly to the Rs 12,641 Cr backlog and confirms Thermax's positioning in India's thermal power capacity expansion. This is a 2-3 year execution contract. (2) Data center cooling entry — Rs 200 Cr in Q3 from US (marquee client) and India; management is excited about this vertical for future growth. India's hyperscale data center capex boom is a genuine secular tailwind. (3) Exactspace Technologies (April 9, 2026) — industrial AI and IoT for predictive maintenance, energy optimization, and factory automation. At Rs 30.48 Cr total deal size this is symbolic, but it sets up a SaaS-embedded services narrative. (4) HPCL MoU (Jan 29, 2026) for HP AEM electrolyzers, CO2 capture, bio-pyrolysis oil — positions Thermax in the nascent green hydrogen and carbon capture market. Electrolyzer manufacturing production targeted mid-2026. (5) FGD legacy: management acknowledged "legacy issues" in FGD and bio-CNG projects — historical FGD orders (e.g., Rs 830 Cr FGD order from 2021) are being executed under cost pressure. This is a drag on near-term margins, not a growth catalyst in the 8-week window.

- **Why The Rally Looks Legit Or Fragile For 1-8 Weeks**: The rally looks partially legitimate and significantly fragile at current price levels. Legitimate because: the Q3 FY26 order intake surge (34% YoY to Rs 3,080 Cr) and Rs 12,641 Cr backlog are hard fundamental anchors; the Rs 1,600 Cr TBWES boiler win and data center cooling orders are real and confirmed; management expects Q4 to be "strong" with margin improvement; the India capex supercycle narrative continues to attract capital into the industrial/capital goods sector. Fragile because: (a) Q4 FY26 results board meeting on May 7, 2026 falls within the Hard Stale window — a miss against Rs 2,900-3,200 Cr revenue consensus or continued OPM compression will re-rate the stock sharply downward; (b) the CMP of Rs 4,141 implies a stock that has run from approximately Rs 2,930 (3-month low extrapolation from 40.97% return) to Rs 4,141 — much of the news is now priced in; (c) at Rs 3,643 on April 10 and Rs 4,141 on April 17, the stock gained another 13.7% in one week — velocity at this level is a warning sign; (d) FII exits over two quarters indicate large institutional funds are distributing, not accumulating.

- **Contradictions, Weaknesses, And Open Risks**:
  1. PAT fell slightly from Rs 643 Cr (FY24) to Rs 627 Cr (FY25) on an annual basis — the profit trajectory on a full-year basis is flat, not expansionary, despite strong order intake.
  2. Q3 FY26 PAT includes Rs 59 Cr in exceptional gains (court win reversal + interest recognition) — underlying operating PAT is Rs 145-150 Cr, not Rs 205 Cr. The 80% headline YoY comparison is misleading.
  3. Chemicals segment: EBITDA underperformed by Rs 50 Cr vs prior year due to new capacity ramp costs and Chinese competition. Management projects recovery to 13-14% EBITDA (from 17% prior peak) — this is a permanent impairment of margins in a segment, not a temporary blip.
  4. FGD and bio-CNG legacy project overruns are ongoing. These are large multi-year contracts and cost overruns are margin dilutive and lumpy.
  5. D/E is rising (0.35x, highest in recent history) — heavy capex cycle is consuming cash. FCF of Rs 143 Cr is thin relative to Rs 49,347 Cr market cap (FCF yield of ~0.3%).
  6. FII shareholding declined 366 bps over two consecutive quarters — this is a material signal of institutional distribution at elevated valuations.
  7. Stock has rallied 13.7% in a single week (April 10–17, 2026) on top of a 40.97% 3-month run — short-term velocity at this level is unsustainable without an immediate earnings catalyst.
  8. P/E of 77x versus sector peer L&T at 25-34x — the premium is extreme and difficult to justify solely on order intake momentum.

- **Peer Placement Notes**: Within a capital goods / industrial machinery momentum screen, THERMAX occupies a premium-multiple position — arguably the most expensive on P/E among capital goods companies despite being a 9% OPM business. It belongs in a category of "expensive on trailing earnings, but order book-supported for multi-year revenue visibility." Peers with confirmed margin expansion (ABB at 14-15% OPM, Siemens India similarly), stronger FCF generation, or lower P/E for comparable order book momentum should rank above THERMAX. Peers with weaker order books, no international diversification, no green theme exposure, or lower-quality backlog (pure domestic cyclicals) should rank below it. THERMAX is a high-quality capital goods company that is currently priced for near-perfection — suitable for a momentum swing only if Q4 FY26 results deliver a clean beat without legacy project charges.
